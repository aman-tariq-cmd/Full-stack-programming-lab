 Science
At the Edge of the Known

In laboratories and aboard orbiting observatories, scientists are dismantling old certainties and replacing them with richer, stranger ones. The Standard Model strains under the weight of experimental anomalies; the human genome continues to yield surprises two decades after its first reading.

"The universe is not only stranger than we imagine — it is stranger than we can imagine."

This year's Nobel laureates reflected that spirit: breakthroughs in cryo-electron microscopy and protein folding have changed what it means to "see" biology at the molecular scale.

 Culture
Art, Ideas & Human Expression

Culture is the nervous system of civilisation — it processes trauma, encodes values, and transmits meaning across generations. In 2025, the cultural conversation is loud, fractured, and alive in ways that remind us why human creativity cannot be automated.

From AI-generated imagery challenging authorship to a global renaissance of craft and slow art, audiences are simultaneously overwhelmed and hungry for depth. Streaming fatigue has birthed a new appreciation for the theatrical, the live, and the ephemeral.

"Culture is not a luxury. It is the architecture of the self."
